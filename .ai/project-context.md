# Project Context: Odyssey Counselling

## Why This Brand Exists
Odyssey Counselling serves Turkish-speaking people in the UK who often struggle to find therapy that feels culturally and linguistically safe. For many users, speaking in a second language creates emotional distance. In therapy, that distance can block honesty, nuance, and trust.

## Why Turkish-Language Therapy Matters
- Emotional language is not only vocabulary; it is identity, memory, and family context.
- Many first-generation immigrants process complex experiences across two cultural frameworks.
- Users may fear being misunderstood by providers unfamiliar with Turkish social norms, migration stress, or family dynamics.
- Therapy in the client’s preferred language can reduce cognitive load and improve therapeutic alliance.

## Why Trust Is the Core Product
Users are not browsing casually. They often arrive while anxious, overwhelmed, or uncertain about opening up. The website’s first task is not persuasion; it is emotional de-escalation.

Trust signals must be immediate:
- clear credentials and professional framing
- calm visual design with generous whitespace
- direct, compassionate, non-judgmental language
- visible privacy boundaries and clear contact expectations

## Why Bilingual UX Matters
Bilingual UX is not a toggle feature; it is a trust architecture decision.
- Users may switch language by topic (practical vs emotional).
- Some users read better in English but express feelings in Turkish.
- Translation must preserve therapeutic meaning, not literal phrasing alone.

Future content operations should support parallel language quality control, not machine-only translation.

## Emotional State at Arrival
Common pre-session states:
- hesitation (“Will I be understood?”)
- fear of stigma
- privacy concerns
- urgency combined with indecision
- past disappointment with generic services

UX implication: avoid pressure. Keep pathways clear, gentle, and predictable.

## Homepage Must Communicate in the First Screen
1. This is a professional counselling practice.
2. Turkish-speaking clients are explicitly welcome.
3. The environment is private, calm, and respectful.
4. Taking a first step is simple and low-pressure.
